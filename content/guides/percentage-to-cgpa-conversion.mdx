# How to Convert Percentage to CGPA Easily

If you've ever stared at a scholarship form that asks for your CGPA when you only have a percentage — or vice versa — you're not alone. Percentage-to-CGPA conversion is one of the most searched academic questions among students in South Asia, and the confusion is understandable. Different universities use wildly different formulas, and there is no single global standard.

This guide will walk you through the most common conversion methods step by step, help you understand why the numbers differ across institutions, and give you practical tools to make accurate conversions every time.

---

## Why Doesn't One Formula Work for Everyone?

The short answer: because grading systems aren't universal.

Universities in India, Bangladesh, Pakistan, the US, and Europe each developed their own grading philosophies. Some use a 4.0-point scale, others a 10-point scale, and some simply use raw percentages. Over time, equivalency formulas emerged — but they were designed by individual institutions, not by any international body.

This is why a student at one university might convert 75% to a 3.5 CGPA, while a student at another institution converts the same 75% to a 7.5 CGPA. Neither is wrong — they're just using different scales.

**Key grading scales in use worldwide:**
- **4.0 Scale** — United States, Canada, many private universities globally
- **10-Point Scale** — Most Indian universities (IITs, NITs, DU, etc.)
- **5.0 Scale** — Some Pakistani and Bangladeshi universities
- **Percentage-Based** — Many schools in Bangladesh, Pakistan, and UK-aligned institutions

## Method 1: Converting Percentage to CGPA on a 10-Point Scale

This is the most common conversion in Indian universities and is deceptively simple.

**Formula:**
> CGPA = Percentage ÷ 9.5

**Why 9.5?** The National Board of Examinations and many universities determined that a 10.0 CGPA corresponds to a range of 91–100%, giving an average divisor of approximately 9.5.

### Step-by-Step Example

| Your Percentage | Formula Applied | CGPA (10-Point) |
|---|---|---|
| 85% | 85 ÷ 9.5 | **8.94** |
| 72% | 72 ÷ 9.5 | **7.57** |
| 60% | 60 ÷ 9.5 | **6.31** |
| 55% | 55 ÷ 9.5 | **5.78** |

## Method 2: Converting Percentage to GPA on a 4.0 Scale

Converting to a US-style 4.0 scale is trickier because there's no single standard — but the most widely referenced method (used by WES and many global credential evaluators) is:

**Formula:**
> GPA (4.0) = (Percentage − 50) × 4 ÷ 50

Or, more simply, divide your percentage by 25 and subtract 2:

> GPA (4.0) = (Percentage ÷ 25) − 2

### Step-by-Step Example

| Percentage | Formula | GPA (4.0) |
|---|---|---|
| 90% | (90 − 50) × 4 ÷ 50 | **3.20** |
| 80% | (80 − 50) × 4 ÷ 50 | **2.40** |
| 70% | (70 − 50) × 4 ÷ 50 | **1.60** |

> ⚠️ **Important Note:** This formula caps at 4.0 for 100% and gives 0 for 50%. If your percentage is below 50, the formula produces a negative number — which indicates a failing grade.

## Method 3: Converting Percentage to CGPA on a 5.0 Scale

Used at several universities in Pakistan and Bangladesh:

**Formula:**
> CGPA (5.0) = Percentage ÷ 20

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Converting grades sounds mechanical, but students frequently make these errors:

1. **Using the wrong scale for your institution.** Always check your university's official grading policy first.
2. **Applying a formula designed for a different country.** An Indian 10-point formula doesn't apply to a US 4.0 transcript.
3. **Rounding too early.** Carry decimal places through the formula and only round the final result.
4. **Ignoring grade boundaries.** Some universities use a 9.5 divisor, others use 10.0. Even a small difference changes your result.

## FAQs

**Q: Is there an official percentage-to-CGPA formula set by the government?**
A: No single official formula exists internationally. India's CBSE board recommends the 9.5 divisor for their exams, but individual universities set their own policies. Always check with your institution.

**Q: My university uses a 7-point scale. How do I convert?**
A: Divide your percentage by the maximum grade (7), then multiply by 7. For example: 70% ÷ 100 × 7 = 4.9 on a 7-point scale. Always verify with your registrar.

**Q: If my percentage is 90%, is my CGPA always a 4.0?**
A: Not necessarily. On a 10-point scale, 90% ÷ 9.5 ≈ 9.47. On a 4.0 scale using the WES formula, it comes to approximately 3.20. The result depends entirely on which scale is being used.
